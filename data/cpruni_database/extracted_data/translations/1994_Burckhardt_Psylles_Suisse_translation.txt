1. Introduction;

The Psyllids form a superfamily of Sternorrhycha whose
members are exclusively plant sap suckers. Most
species are highly specific to their host plants
unlike species of Aleyrodoidea, a superfamily
phylogenetically close. Meyer-Diir was the first to try to treat in a way
the psyllids of the Swiss fauna. With the exception of one article
preliminary (Meyer-Diir, 1871) mentioning 17 species, this project
was never realized. Schaefer (1949) listed 95 species of Switzerland in
reviewing data from the literature and based on material
important from the North-West of Switzerland collected by himself and on the
collection of Canon Cerutti including mainly Valais material. The last list of Swiss Psyllids finally is that of Burckhardt
(1983) which listed 129 species. Since then, more material has been collected by several
people, thus supplementing the incomplete knowledge of the fauna
Swiss. Some of these data are presented in this note. I
would like to thank Y. Basset (Geneva), M. Brancucci (Basel), P.
Hattenschwiler (Uster), C. Lienhard (Geneva), B. Merz (Ziirich) and J.
Steffen (Geneva) who generously contributed & this work with
donations or loans of specimens, The material is deposited at the Museum
d'histoire naturelle, Geneva, unless otherwise indicated. 109
