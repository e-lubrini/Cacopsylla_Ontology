Psyllids (Hemiptera: Psylloidea) are small phytophagous insect, ranging from 1-10 mm. About 4,000 species are known worldwide (Li, 2011; Burckhardt & Ouvrard, 2012). Some
species are important pests of crops and forest trees, damaging plants by direct feeding and
vectoring plant diseases. They are generally host specific, and related psyllid species often
develop on related host taxa (Ouvrard et al., 2015). Three species described by A. Foerster (1848) have been misidentified by Z.A. Konova-
lova (1988). In this paper, these misidentifications of psyllids from the Russian Far East are
reviewed, and one species is recorded from this region for the first time. Material for this study
