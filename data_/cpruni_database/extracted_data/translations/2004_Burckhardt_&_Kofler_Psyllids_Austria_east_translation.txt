Abstracts
Further records of Psylloidea from Eastern Tyrol, Carinthia and Burgenland
Forty-two psyllid species (Psyllidae 26, Triozidae 16) are recorded from the district of Lienz, 17 of
which for the first time, increasing the number of psyllid species known from East Tyrol to 55. eight
psyllid species are recorded from Carinthia and one from Burgenland: Bactericera trigonica which is
new for Austria, The present paper illustrates the importance of continued field work for obtaining a
Detailed faunal picture. summary

For the relatively small district of Lienz (East Tyrol), 42 species of leaf fleas are reported (26 Psyllidae,
16 Triozidae), of which 17 species are new finds. This means that 55 species are currently out
known to East Tyrol. Eight species are named for the state of Carinthia. For the state of Burgenland only one:
Bactericera trigonica, this is new to Austria. This work shows how important it is regularly
field work carried out is to obtain a reasonably realistic faunistic picture. introduction

Leaf fleas or leaf suckers form a small group of very host-specific ones
Plant lice (Sternorrhyncha) with more than 3000 species described worldwide
almost 400 of which have been registered from Europe. Various species are on cultivated plants
schidlich (BURCKHARDT 1994) and are often spread with them. With the
globalized trade, there has been a strong increase in exotic species. So e.g. three new exotic flea species in the last two years
Discovered Switzerland (BURCKHARDT & MUHLETHALER 2004). For such changes
To be able to recognize the fauna, good faunistic basics are essential. This work is a contribution to improving the knowledge of
